Autism In Toddlers

Autism, a neurodevelopmental disorder, can manifest in toddlers as early as age 2. It's characterized by challenges in social interaction, communication, and repetitive behaviors. Toddlers with autism may struggle with making eye contact, engaging in reciprocal play, and demonstrating limited speech or difficulty expressing their needs. They often exhibit strong preferences for routine and may become upset by changes. Early detection and intervention are crucial for better outcomes, as therapies and support can help toddlers with autism develop essential social and communication skills, improving their overall quality of life and future prospects. Parents should consult with healthcare professionals if they have concerns.
